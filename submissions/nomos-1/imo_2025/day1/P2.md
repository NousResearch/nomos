# P2

## Problem

Let \(\Omega\) and \(\Gamma\) be circles with centres \(M\) and \(N\), respectively, such that the radius of \(\Omega\) is less than the radius of \(\Gamma\). Suppose \(\Omega\) and \(\Gamma\) intersect at two distinct points \(A\) and \(B\). Line \(MN\) intersects \(\Omega\) at \(C\) and \(\Gamma\) at \(D\), so that \(C, M, N, D\) lie on \(MN\) in that order. Let \(P\) be the circumcenter of triangle \(ACD\). Line \(AP\) meets \(\Omega\) again at \(E \ne A\) and meets \(\Gamma\) again at \(F \ne A\). Let \(H\) be the orthocenter of triangle \(PMN\).

Prove that the line through \(H\) parallel to \(AP\) is tangent to the circumcircle of triangle \(BEF\).

## Submission

We place the figure in a coordinate system with \(M=(0,0)\) and \(N=(d,0)\), \(d=MN\). Let \(\Omega:\;x^2+y^2=r^2\) and \(\Gamma:\;(x-d)^2+y^2=R^2\) with \(r<R\). Their intersections are \(A=(h,k)\) and \(B=(h,-k)\) (with \(k>0\)). Then
\[
h=\frac{d^2+r^2-R^2}{2d},\qquad k^2=r^2-h^2.
\]
The line \(MN\) meets \(\Omega\) again at \(C=(-r,0)\) and \(\Gamma\) again at \(D=(d+R,0)\); the order \(C,M,N,D\) is preserved because \(-r<0<d<d+R\).

Define the positive numbers
\[
S=d+R+r,\quad a=R-r,\quad b=d+R-r,\quad c=d+r-R,\quad t=R+r-d.
\]
A standard factorisation gives
\[
k^2=\frac{Sbct}{4d^2},\qquad h+r=\frac{cS}{2d}.
\]

---

### 1. Coordinates of \(P\) and the vector \(\overrightarrow{AP}\)

The circumcenter \(P\) of \(\triangle ACD\) lies on the perpendicular bisector of \(CD\), i.e. \(x=\frac{b}{2}\). Let \(P=\bigl(\frac b2,q\bigr)\). From \(PA=PC\) we obtain
\[
q=-\frac{p(h+r)}{k}=-\frac{b c S}{4kd},
\]
so \(P=\bigl(\frac b2,-\frac{b c S}{4kd}\bigr)\). Set
\[
v=P-A=(v_x,v_y),\qquad v_x=\frac b2-h=\frac{aS}{2d},\qquad
v_y=-\frac{b c S}{4kd}-k=-\frac{S(R+r)bc}{4d^2k}.
\]

A direct computation using \(k^2=\frac{Sbct}{4d^2\) and the identities \(bc=d^2-a^2,\;(R+r)^2=a^2+4Rr\) yields
\[
|v|^2=\frac{a^2S^2}{4d^2}+\frac{S^2(R+r)^2b^2c^2}{16d^4k^2}=\frac{S R r}{t}.
\]

---

### 2. Orthocenter \(H\) of \(\triangle PMN\)

Since \(M,N\) lie on the \(x\)-axis, the altitude from \(P\) is vertical; thus \(H\) has \(x\)-coordinate \(\frac b2\). Using the altitude from \(M\) we find
\[
H=\Bigl(\frac b2,\;-\frac{kd}{S}\Bigr).
\]

---

### 3. Points \(E\) and \(F\)

Parametrise \(AP\) as \(A+\lambda v\). Substituting into \(\Omega\) and \(\Gamma\) gives the non‑zero parameters
\[
\lambda_E=\frac{t}{R},\qquad \lambda_F=\frac{t}{r},
\]
so
\[
E=A+\frac{t}{R}v,\qquad F=A+\frac{t}{r}v.
\]

---

### 4. Circumcenter \(O\) of \(\triangle BEF\)

Let \(O=(x,y)\). From \(|OE|=|OF\) we obtain
\[
v_x(x-h)+v_y(y-k)=\frac{(R+r)S}{2}. \tag{1}
\]
Using \(|OB|=|OE|\) and (1) together with \(|v|^2=\frac{S R r}{t}\) we get
\[
4ky+tS=0\quad\Longrightarrow\quad y=-\frac{tS}{4k}. \tag{2}
\]
Inserting (2) into (1) and simplifying (using \(k^2=\frac{Sbct}{4d^2\)) yields
\[
x-h=\frac{a(R+r}{2d},
\]
hence
\[
x=\frac b2-\frac a2.
\]
Thus
\[
O=\Bigl(\frac b2-\frac a2,\;-\frac{tS}{4k}\Bigr).
\]

---

### 5. Distance from \(O\) to the line through \(H\) parallel to \(AP\)

We have
\[
O-H=\Bigl(-\frac a2,\;-\frac{tS}{4k}+\frac{kd}{S}\Bigr).
\]
The cross product with \(v\) is
\[
(O-H)\times v=\Bigl(-\frac a2\Bigr)v_y-\Bigl(-\frac{tS}{4k}+\frac{kd}{S}\Bigr)v_x.
\]
Substituting \(v_x,v_y\) and simplifying (using \(k^2=\frac{Sbct}{4d^2\) and \(bc+tS=4Rr\)) gives
\[
(O-H)\times v=\frac{a R r S}{2kd}. \tag{3}
\]
Hence the distance is
\[
\operatorname{dist}=\frac{|(O-H)\times v|}{|v|}=\frac{a R r S}{2kd\,|v|}.
\]

---

### 6. Radius of \(\odot(BEF)\)

\[
x-h=\frac{a(R+r}{2d},\qquad y+k=-\frac{tS}{4k}+k=-\frac{a^2 t S}{4d^2k},
\]
so
\[
R_c^2=|OB|^2=\frac{a^2(R+r)^2}{4d^2}+\frac{a^4t^2S^2}{16d^4k^2}=\frac{a^2(R+r)^2}{4d^2}+\frac{a^4t^2S^2}{16d^4k^2}.
\]

---

### 7. Verification that the line is tangent

Using \(k^2=\frac{Sbct}{4d^2\) and \(|v|^2=\frac{S R r}{t}\) we compute
\[
\operatorname{dist}^2=\frac{a^2R^2r^2S^2}{4k^2d^2|v|^2}=\frac{a^2R^2r^2S}{bct},
\]
\[
|v|^2R_c^2=\frac{S R r}{t}\Bigl(\frac{a^2(R+r)^2}{4d^2}+\frac{a^4t^2S^2}{16d^4k^2}\Bigr)
=\frac{a^2R r}{4d^2}\Bigl(\frac{S(R+r)^2}{t}+\frac{a^2S^2}{bc}\Bigr).
\]
Equality \(\operatorname{dist}^2=R_c^2\) is equivalent to
\[
\frac{4d^2Rr}{bc\,t}=\frac{S(R+r)^2}{t}+\frac{a^2S^2}{bc}.
\]
Multiplying by \(bc\,t\) and using \(bc=d^2-a^2,\;St=(R+r)^2-d^2\) gives
\[
4d^2Rr=(d^2-a^2)(R+r)^2+a^2\bigl((R+r)^2-d^2\bigr)=d^2\bigl((R+r)^2-a^2\bigr)=4d^2Rr,
\]
which holds. Therefore \(\operatorname{dist}=R_c\); the line through \(H\) parallel to \(AP\) is tangent to the circumcircle of \(BEF\). ∎
